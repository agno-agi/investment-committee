# Tesla, Inc. (TSLA)

## Business Overview
Tesla designs, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles (EVs), energy storage systems, and solar products. The company is also developing autonomous driving technology (Full Self-Driving) and humanoid robots (Optimus). Tesla is the world's most valuable automaker by market capitalization.

## Revenue Segments
- **Automotive:** Electric vehicles (~85% of revenue). Model 3/Y are volume sellers; Cybertruck is the newest addition. Includes regulatory credit sales to other automakers.
- **Energy Generation & Storage:** Megapack (utility-scale batteries), Powerwall (residential), solar panels. Fastest-growing segment by percentage.
- **Services & Other:** Vehicle service, supercharging network, merchandise, insurance.

## Competitive Position
Tesla has brand recognition, manufacturing scale, and a Supercharger network that competitors are now adopting (NACS standard). Its vertically integrated approach (battery manufacturing, software, charging) provides cost advantages. However, competition has intensified significantly from legacy automakers and Chinese EV makers.

## Growth Drivers
- Full Self-Driving (FSD) progress toward Level 4 autonomy (robotaxi potential)
- Energy storage business growing rapidly as utility-scale demand increases
- Next-generation affordable vehicle platform targeting sub-$30K price point
- Optimus humanoid robot program (long-term moonshot)
- Expansion of manufacturing capacity (Gigafactory Mexico, Shanghai expansion)
