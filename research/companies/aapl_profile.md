# Apple Inc. (AAPL)

## Business Overview
Apple designs, manufactures, and markets consumer electronics, software, and services. The company's ecosystem of hardware (iPhone, Mac, iPad, Apple Watch, AirPods) and services (App Store, iCloud, Apple Music, Apple TV+, Apple Pay) creates one of the most loyal customer bases in technology.

## Revenue Segments
- **iPhone:** Largest segment (~50% of revenue). Upgrade cycles driven by camera improvements, chip performance, and increasingly AI features.
- **Services:** Fastest-growing segment (~25% of revenue) with high margins. Includes App Store, iCloud, Apple Music, Apple TV+, Apple Pay, AppleCare.
- **Mac:** Personal computers powered by Apple Silicon (M-series chips).
- **iPad:** Tablets for consumer and education markets.
- **Wearables & Accessories:** Apple Watch, AirPods, Vision Pro headset.

## Growth Drivers
- Apple Intelligence (on-device AI) driving iPhone upgrade cycle
- Services revenue growth with improving margins
- India market expansion as a growth engine
- Vision Pro and spatial computing platform
- Apple Silicon enabling competitive enterprise Mac adoption

## Key Risks
- iPhone saturation in mature markets
- Regulatory pressure on App Store fees (EU Digital Markets Act, DOJ antitrust)
- China exposure: ~20% of revenue, geopolitical tension risk
- Services growth deceleration as base matures
- Competition in AI features from Google/Samsung Android ecosystem

## Recent Developments
- Apple Intelligence suite launched with on-device AI processing
- iPhone sales stabilized after upgrade cycle concerns
- Services segment continues to set revenue records with expanding margins
- Increased focus on India manufacturing and market expansion
